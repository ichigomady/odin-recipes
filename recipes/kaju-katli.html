Kaju Katli

Kaju katli, also known as kaju barfi, is a traditional Indian dessert characterized by its diamond shape, made with cashew nuts, sugar, cardamom powder, and ghee butter. This sweet treat often comes wrapped in an edible silver foil, signifying luxury and appreciation for the consumer.

It is traditionally consumed during the Diwali festival, but it is also an ideal gift for friends and family on special occasions. Kaju katli is often prepared during Ganesh Chaturthi, a festival celebrating the birth of Ganesha, the elephant god.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups chilled Cashew nut, powder
  • 3/4 cup Water
  • 3/4 cup Sugar
  • 1/2 tsp Cardamom powder
  • 1 tsp Rose water
  • 1 tsp Ghee
  • Silver vark

Steps:

  1. In a small mixer jar, add cashews, grind to smooth powder. keep aside.
  2. Now, sieve the ground cashew powder.
  3. Now in a deep non stick pan, add water, sugar, cardamom powder.
  4. Keep the flame on low and keep on stirring to form syrup.
  5. Stir the sugar syrup till it forms string consistency.
  6. Now, add the sieve cashew powder into the sugar syrup.
  7. Once the cashew paste turns to dough, leave the pan and add ghee.
  8. Continue to mix till the ghee melts completely and cashew dough is formed leaving the pan.
  9. Switch off the flame. Grease the butter paper with ghee.
  10. Transfer the cashew dough onto the butter paper.
  11. Once you get smooth dough, cover with butter paper.
  12. Take a tray and press to form a uniform layer. Spread some saffron strands.
  13. Then roll the dough with a rolling-pin, adjusting the thickness according to your choice.
  14. Now, cut the dough into diamond shape or the shape you wish for.
